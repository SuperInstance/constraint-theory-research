**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-03-16
**Status:** Active

## 1. Overview

This document establishes guidelines for author contributions to Constraint Theory research papers, ensuring transparency, proper attribution, and adherence to academic standards.

---

## 2. Authorship Criteria

### 2.1 Qualifying for Authorship

To be listed as an author, a contributor must meet **all** of the following criteria (ICMJE guidelines):

1. **Substantial Contribution**: Made substantial contributions to:
   - Conception and design of the research, OR
   - Acquisition, analysis, or interpretation of data, OR
   - Development of algorithms or implementations

2. **Drafting or Revision**: Participated in:
   - Drafting the paper, OR
   - Critically revising it for important intellectual content

3. **Final Approval**: Approved the final version to be published

4. **Accountability**: Agrees to be accountable for all aspects of the work

### 2.2 Non-Qualifying Contributions

The following **do not** qualify for authorship alone:

- Providing funding or resources
- General supervision
- Administrative support
- Data collection without intellectual contribution
- Technical editing only
- Peer review feedback

Such contributions should be acknowledged in the Acknowledgments section.

## 3. Contribution Categories

### 3.1 Conceptualization

- Development of research ideas
- Formulation of research questions

- Formal definitions

- Code optimization

**CRediT Term**: Validation

**CRediT Term**: Writing - original draft / Writing - review & editing

### 3.6 Resources

- Computing infrastructure

**CRediT Term**: Resources

## 4. Author Order

### 4.1 Primary Authors

**First Author**: The person who:
- Made the most substantial intellectual contribution
- Wrote the majority of the paper
- Takes primary responsibility for the work

**Co-First Authors**: When contributions are equal, both may be designated as co-first authors.

### 4.2 Middle Authors

Listed in order of decreasing contribution. Order should be determined by:

1. Size of intellectual contribution
2. Amount of work invested
3. Chronological involvement

### 4.3 Last Author

**Senior/Corresponding Author**: Typically the PI or project lead who:
- Secured funding
- Provided supervision
- Takes responsibility for correspondence
- Ensures integrity of the work

### 4.4 Alphabetical Order

For papers with many authors where determining relative contribution is difficult, middle authors may be listed alphabetically.

## 5. CRediT Taxonomy

We use the [Contributor Roles Taxonomy (CRediT)](https://credit.niso.org/) for standardized author contribution statements.

### 5.1 Standard Roles

| Role | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Conceptualization | Ideas; formulation of research goals |
| Data curation | Management activities to annotate data |
| Formal analysis | Application of statistical, mathematical techniques |
| Funding acquisition | Acquisition of financial support |
| Investigation | Conducting research process |
| Methodology | Development of methodology |
| Project administration | Management and coordination |
| Resources | Provision of study materials |
| Software | Programming, software development |
| Supervision | Oversight and leadership |
| Validation | Verification of results |
| Visualization | Preparation of figures/tables |
| Writing – original draft | Initial preparation of paper |
| Writing – review & editing | Critical review and revisions |

### 5.2 Example Contribution Statement

```markdown
## Author Contributions

**Alice Smith**: Conceptualization, Formal analysis, Writing – original draft
**Bob Jones**: Software, Validation, Writing – review & editing
**Carol Chen**: Investigation, Visualization
**David Lee**: Resources, Supervision
**Eve Wilson**: Funding acquisition, Project administration, Writing – review & editing

All authors approved the final manuscript.
```

## 6. Changes to Authorship

### 6.1 Before Submission

Authorship changes require agreement from all authors. Submit a written request with:
- Reason for change
- All authors' signatures (electronic OK)

### 6.2 After Submission

- **During review**: Contact the editor immediately
- **After acceptance**: Rare; requires editorial approval
- **After publication**: Erratum or corrigendum required

---

## 7. Collaborative Authorship

### 7.1 Consortium Authors

For large collaborations:

1. List consortium name as author
2. Include individual names in Acknowledgments
3. Specify in author notes how to cite

Example:
```
The Constraint Theory Consortium: [followed by list of members in appendix]
```

### 7.2 Institutional Authors

When crediting institutions:
- Use standard institutional name
- Include ORCID for institution if available
- Specify contact person

---

## 8. Author Identification

### 8.1 ORCID

**Strongly encouraged** for all authors:
- Unique identifier
- Free registration
- Links all publications
- Required by many journals

Register at: https://orcid.org/

### 8.2 Author Affiliations

Format:
```
Name¹, Name²
¹ Department, Institution, City, Country
² Department, Institution, City, Country
```

Rules:
- Use current affiliation for correspondence
- Previous affiliations marked with "Present address:"
- Multiple affiliations numbered consistently

---

## 9. Corresponding Author Responsibilities

### 9.1 Primary Responsibilities

1. **Communication**: Handle all correspondence with editors/reviewers
2. **Availability**: Ensure availability throughout review process
3. **Integrity**: Ensure all authors agree on content
4. **Updates**: Keep co-authors informed of progress
5. **Post-Publication**: Handle any queries or corrections

### 9.2 Multiple Corresponding Authors

Allowed when:
- Large collaborations
- Geographic distribution
- Different expertise areas

All corresponding authors must be listed in the manuscript.

---

## 10. Acknowledgments

### 10.1 Who to Acknowledge

- Funding sources (with grant numbers)
- Technical assistance
- Administrative support
- Data providers
- Reviewers (if acknowledged by journal)
- Previous presenters of work

### 10.2 Format

```markdown
## Acknowledgments

This work was supported by [Funding Agency] under Grant [Number].
We thank [Name] for [contribution].
We acknowledge [Institution] for providing [resource].
```

## 11. Conflict of Interest

### 11.1 Disclosure Requirements

All authors must disclose:

1. **Financial Interests**:
   - Employment relationships
   - Consultant arrangements
   - Stock ownership
   - Patent holdings
   - Research grants

2. **Personal Relationships**:
   - Family relationships with co-authors
   - Previous collaboration conflicts

3. **Intellectual Property**:
   - Pending patents
   - Licensed technology

### 11.2 Statement Format

```markdown
## Conflicts of Interest

Author A is an employee of Company X.
Author B holds patent P related to this work.
Author C has no conflicts to declare.
```

## 12. Authorship Disputes

### 12.1 Prevention

- Discuss authorship early in project
- Revisit decisions as project evolves
- Document all contributions

### 12.2 Resolution Process

1. **Internal Discussion**: All parties discuss
2. **Mediation**: Neutral third party if needed
3. **Institutional**: Involve department/research office
4. **External**: COPE guidelines for formal disputes

### 12.3 Resources

- [COPE Guidelines](https://publicationethics.org/)
- [ICMJE Recommendations](http://www.icmje.org/)
- Institutional research integrity office

---

## 13. Checklist for Authors

Before submission, verify:

### Authorship
- [ ] All contributors meeting authorship criteria included
- [ ] Non-qualifying contributors in Acknowledgments
- [ ] Author order agreed by all
- [ ] Corresponding author designated
- [ ] Affiliations current and correct

### Documentation
- [ ] CRediT contributions assigned
- [ ] ORCID IDs collected
- [ ] Email addresses current
- [ ] Conflict of interest disclosed

### Agreement
- [ ] All authors read final manuscript
- [ ] All authors approve submission
- [ ] All authors agree to be accountable
- [ ] Copyright transfer/licensing agreed
