### Appendix: Assumptions and Limitations

This analysis is subject to several limitations that should be considered when interpreting results. First, the experiments were conducted in simulated environments that may not fully capture real-world noise and variability. Second, the multi-agent deliberation process relies on LLM-based agents whose outputs may contain inherent biases. Third, the performance benchmarks were measured on specific hardware configurations and may vary across different computational platforms. Fourth, the rigidity percolation results (H5) showed unexpected deviation from theoretical predictions, warranting additional investigation. Finally, the 12-language cultural synthesis, while comprehensive, does not represent all possible mathematical traditions.

Future work should address these limitations through: (1) real-world deployment studies; (2) human expert validation of agent deliberations; (3) cross-platform performance benchmarking; (4) dedicated rigidity percolation experiments; and (5) expansion to additional cultural and mathematical traditions. The research team is actively pursuing these directions to strengthen the empirical foundation of the holographic membrane dynamics framework.
